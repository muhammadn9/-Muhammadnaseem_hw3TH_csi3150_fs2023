Is a trip to Alaska on your list?

Alaska, the northernmost and largest state in the United States, is a land of unparalleled natural beauty and adventure. With its towering mountains, pristine glaciers, and vast wilderness, it's a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts. From the rugged terrain of Denali National Park to the icy fjords of the Inside Passage, Alaska offers a diverse range of experiences. Wildlife thrives here, including bears, whales, and bald eagles. Visitors can witness the spectacular Northern Lights in the winter and enjoy the midnight sun during the summer months. Alaska's rich native culture, gold rush history, and resilient communities add depth to its fascinating story. Whether you're exploring its national parks, embarking on a cruise, or simply enjoying the solitude of its wilderness, Alaska promises unforgettable adventures and memories that will last a lifetime.

Alaska's rugged terrain is home to Denali National Park, where the mighty Denali mountain stands as North America's highest peak. The state's stunning coastline features pristine fjords, and its pristine wilderness offers opportunities for hiking, wildlife watching, and adventure.

The indigenous cultures of Alaska, including the Inupiat and Yupik peoples, contribute to the state's rich heritage. Whether witnessing the Northern Lights or exploring the historic Gold Rush trails, Alaska is a land of discovery and wonder.

Your Bears, trains and Icebergs adventures starts with a scenic ride on the Alaska Railroad's Glacier Discovery train. The train departs from the Anchorage Depot located at 411 West First Avenue at 9:45am. After traveling through the anchorage you will descend through the Anchorage Coastal Wildlife Refuge.

What to wear

In Alaska, dressing in layers is the key to staying comfortable. The weather can be quite variable, so pack clothing that can adapt to changing conditions. A warm, waterproof jacket, insulated boots, and moisture-wicking base layers are essential. Don't forget accessories like gloves and a hat. Even in the summer, temperatures can fluctuate, so be prepared for chilly mornings and potentially warmer afternoons. And if you plan on exploring the great outdoors, reliable rain gear is a must to keep you dry during unexpected showers.

Reservations

When planning a trip to Alaska, it's advisable to make reservations for accommodations, activities, and tours well in advance, especially during the peak tourist season from May to September. This ensures you secure your desired lodging and activities, which can fill up quickly. Whether you're booking a cozy cabin in the wilderness, a guided wildlife tour, or a scenic train ride, reserving ahead of time is the best way to guarantee a smooth and enjoyable Alaskan adventure. Be flexible with your travel dates, as it can increase your chances of securing reservations and allow you to fully experience the incredible beauty and activities that Alaska has to offer.
